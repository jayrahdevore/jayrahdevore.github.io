# Radios

I've been eyeing a couple of toy radio options.

## QDX Transciever
- The [QDX Transciever](http://shop.qrp-labs.com/qdx):
	This is a computer controlled HF (country/worldwide) radio that is very limited but gets me on the air and potentially talking 100s to 1000s of miles.
	I'd be nice to have the enclosure (+$20).
	They sell two models: a high band [160-20m] and low band [20-10m] model.
	If you're interested in picking up one of these, I'd also need an antenna big enough for it (or the NanoVNA mentioned above to build my own with scrap).
	- The [NanoVNA Antenna analyzer](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07Z5VY7B6) mentioned above so I can build an antenna from parts
	- An [End-fed half wave](https://qrpguys.com/end-fed-half-wave-sota-antenna-tuner) should offer pretty good performance, but I have no idea how I'd hang a wire long enough.  If you purchase this one, I'd prefer the high band model of radio.
	- A [Vertical antenna](https://qrpguys.com/ds1-antenna) would require the high band [20/17/../10m] version of the QDX.  Not as good of a performer but easy to deploy at a park (probably my pick)

- [TYT MD-UV380 DMR radio](https://www.ebay.com/itm/154579278532): This is a pretty cool radio that I'd probably take apart and hack around with it.  I've never tried DMR radio

- [Lilygo T-ECHO](https://www.aliexpress.us/item/3256802656141638.html): A really neat project that I've been tinkering with lately.  I have a different model that I can use with this one to build a mesh network.  Unfortunately, Aliexpress seems to be the place to buy these.
